# OpenRail Technical Committee Summary Report 2026 Q1

The first quarter of 2026 marked a period of governance consolidation and strategic planning for the Technical Committee (TC), with a strong focus on project governance frameworks, community growth, and external engagement.

**Florian Amsallem** was re-elected as chair of the Technical Committee for a twelve-month term (February 1, 2026 to January 31, 2027), demonstrating continuity in leadership and the committee's confidence in his direction.

A major focus of Q1 was advancing **project governance**. The TC initiated workshops and discussions to address challenges arising from collaboration of different companies within projects, where interests of project and how they are used in products have to be aligned. The team identified key resources for developing governance frameworks, including the Apache Software Foundation's model and OpenRail's own open-sourcing practices documentation. A governance draft for **Netzgrafik-Editor (NGE)** was reviewed and submitted as a pull request, marking concrete progress in formalizing project governance structures.

The TC continued its commitment to **transparency and reporting**. Work progressed on the 2025 annual report, with projects encouraged to focus on specific achievements rather than general descriptions. Additionally, the TC introduced a new initiative to produce **quarterly metrics reports** with consistent structure, to be published on the organization's website, providing regular insights into OpenRail's progress.

On the **infrastructure side**, the **OSRD demo setup** received positive feedback, functioning well with minimal maintenance requirements and proving cost-effective. However, usage metrics are not yet in place, though the OSRD team may address this in the future.

The TC took significant steps toward **community growth and external engagement**. The committee registered the OpenRail Association for **Google Summer of Code 2026**, recognizing the program's potential for attracting students to open-source contributions and increasing visibility. The application was not successful for 2026, we will revisit in 2027.

A workshop proposal was discussed to share experiences on leveraging sovereignty as an opportunity for open source adoption and to develop strategies for growing the community. The TC also explored how to better communicate about open source within companies, with a focus on transitioning existing projects toward open source and securing funding for open source initiatives.

The TC successfully **reused material from OpenRail Day**, with all presentations made available for sharing on the website and other purposes. The event attracted approximately 500 participants, demonstrating strong interest in OpenRail's work.

Finally, the TC began discussions on establishing an **AI policy for AI-generated contributions**, reviewing existing policies from organizations like the Linux Foundation before developing OpenRail's own guidelines.

With strengthened governance processes, new community engagement initiatives, and continued focus on transparency, the TC begins 2026 well-positioned to expand OpenRail's impact across the rail sector.
